FAQ about our German proxies

How many German proxies do you have in your proxy pools?

The number of German proxies we have in our pools varies from month to month with changes in demand. However, we always have hundreds of thousands of German IPs available and can scale up quickly if demand increases.

Are your German proxies datacenter IPs, residential IPs or mobile IPs?

We have German datacenter IPs, residential IPs and mobile IPs in our German proxy pools. When using standard requests your requests will be routed through our German datacenter IPs (with German residential IPs as backups), however, if you add premium=true to your request then all your requests will be sent through our premium German residential and mobile proxy pools.

How can I access your German proxy list?

To use our German proxies you simply need to send your requests to the API via our API endpoint or proxy port along with the country_code=de flag. From here the API will automatically route the request to our German proxy pools and rotate proxies after every request.

Can I use your German IPs for my VPN or to log into Netflix, BBC iPlayer, Amazon Prime?

Our German proxies are designed for web scraping German websites that display different data depending on the IP used to request the page. So they aren’t a good option to use with VPNs or streaming websites.

Can I test your German proxies before subscribing to a plan?

Yes, you can sign up to our free plan here and make up to 1,000 requests with German IPs by adding the parameter country_code=de to your request.
